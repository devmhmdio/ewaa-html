Message From Our Director General

Ewaa is here today because of the seeds of hope planted by Her Highness Sheikha Fatima, the ‘Mother of the Nation’, and nurtured by the vision of the late Sheikh Zayed, the founding father of the UAE. Sheikh Zayed spoke about the importance of the human right to dignity. Every day this spirit guides the work we do.

We started as the nonprofit Ewaa Shelter for Women and Children, and today we are the Abu Dhabi Center for Sheltering and Humanitarian Care – Ewaa, a social sector government entity.

From handling cases of human trafficking since 2008 to handling all forms of violence from 2020, we have grown into thriving trees that spread their shade over those in need.

Over the years, we have nurtured what we, at Ewaa, refer to as our 360° Model of Care. We work directly with the people who carry the burden of violence so they can heal and rejoin society. We support national efforts towards violence prevention, and advocate for policy-level changes so we can tackle violence more effectively.

As we grow, our shade continues to provide respite to more and more vulnerable people – women, men, children, from our Emirati and expat communities in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi.

On behalf of all of us at Ewaa, I encourage you to do your part. Join us to plant more seeds of awareness and break this cycle of violence in our society.

— Her Excellency Sarah Shuhail

Her Excellency Sarah Shuhail

Director-General

Her Excellency Sarah Ibrahim Shuhail is the Director-General of the Abu Dhabi Center for Shelter and Humanitarian Care - Ewaa, an affiliate of the Department of Community Development.

HE Sarah has led Ewaa since its establishment in 2008. Ewaa’s scope of work has expanded significantly under her leadership, especially in 2020, when the Executive Council of the Emirate of Abu Dhabi granted it more responsibilities and powers.

The decision was a result of Ewaa’s successes and its role in providing services to hundreds of victims, solving their cases, rehabilitating them, and enabling them to effectively return to society.

Ewaa has now moved beyond sheltering people who are victims of human trafficking to include care, rehabilitation, and empowerment of various cases of violence.

HE Sarah plays an active role in developing policies and laws related to the upholding of human rights. She is a member of several relevant committees, including:

  • • The National Committee to Combat Human Trafficking
  • • The Committee to Study and Amend Federal Law 51 of 2006 on Human Trafficking, affiliated to the National Committee to Combat Human Trafficking
  • • The Standing Committee for the Follow-up to the Universal Periodic Review on Human Rights 2010
  • • The Higher Committee for Child Protection at the Ministry of Interior

HE Sarah has won several awards in recognition of her efforts, including the Petrochem GR8 Women Awards (2012) for work in the humanitarian field, and the Most Inspirational Woman (2012) at the L'Officiel Arab Woman Awards for her achievements in rehabilitating victims of human trafficking. She has also been featured on the list of the most powerful Arab women in the magazine ‘Arabian Business’, and in CEO Magazine as one of the 100 most powerful Arab women.

HE Sarah’s early career was dedicated to building and developing people. A former educator with more than 31 years of experience in the country, she has worked as a teacher in schools in Abu Dhabi. She went on to become a director of various schools in the emirate, and an educational consultant.

HE Sarah holds a diploma in school administration from the United Arab Emirates University, and a diploma in combating human trafficking from the Dubai Judicial Institute. She has also been certified as an ‘expert’ in the field of human trafficking by the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime.

Maitha Ghanim Al Mazrouei

Manager - Strategic Affairs and Communication

Maitha Ghanim Al Mazrouei is Strategic Affairs and Communication Manager at the Abu Dhabi Center for Shelter and Humanitarian Care - Ewaa.

Maitha is responsible for Ewaa’s strategic planning and policies in line with the direction of the social sector in Abu Dhabi. She is also responsible for developing appropriate means of institutional communication according to the approved strategic plans.

Since joining Ewaa in 2009, she has represented the entity and the UAE at several regional and international forums. She is a regular participant in workshops organized by the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime in the GCC and internationally.

Maitha was a member of the official UAE delegation that carried out the Universal Periodic Review of human rights as part of the agenda of the Human Rights Council in Geneva in 2018. She is a regular participant in meetings and activities of the international forum Bali Process to discuss developments in the UAE related to combating human trafficking crimes and following up international trends in this field.

Maitha also participates in idea exchanges on local and international platforms about the global best practices to protect and assist victims of violence and human trafficking.

Maitha holds a Bachelor of Science in Information Systems from the United Arab Emirates University and an Executive Master of Business Administration from Zayed University. She also holds a Diploma in Combating Human Trafficking Crimes organized by the Dubai Police General Command in cooperation with the National Committee to Combat Human Trafficking.

She has a national expert certificate awarded by the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime as part of its Training of Trainers Program in the field of human trafficking.

Dr Amal Mahmoud Abdel All

Humanitarian Support and Counseling Department Manager

Dr. Amal Mahmoud Abdel All has been working as a legal advisor to the Abu Dhabi Center for Shelter and Humanitarian Care - Ewaa since June 2012. As of 2021, she is entrusted with leading the Humanitarian Support and Counseling Department.

Dr Amal’s primary role is to ensure Ewaa’s regulatory procedures and legal policies are in line with the legislation, laws, and regulations of the UAE, and all regional and global treaties, agreements, and protocols that the state has signed and is committed to as an initiating and active member of the international community.

In her capacity as director of the Department of Humanitarian Support and Consultations, Dr. Amal supervises the case reception, case management, the legal support, and the empowerment departments, including family counseling services, research, and studies.

Dr. Amal has written several working papers and presented them at regional and international forums on human trafficking, human rights, and other legal issues, at which the center has represented the UAE. She has also represented her country, the Republic of Sudan, at meetings of international human rights organizations.

Dr. Amal is a member of several legal bodies and committees in the UAE and Sudan that work on women’s issues, juveniles, victims of violence and abuse, and vulnerable groups in general. She is a member of the supervisory board of the Zayed House for Family Care and the National Committee to Combat Human Trafficking. She is also on the supervisory committee of Durrat Al Emarat magazine, which she helped launch as part of her work with the Abu Dhabi Women’s Development Association.

Dr. Amal was previously assigned to several legal and humanitarian groups in the UAE. She provided her extensive experience and advisory services to the office of Her Highness Sheikha Fatima bint Mubarak, President of the Supreme Council for Motherhood and Childhood, Supreme Chair of the Family Development Foundation, and the ‘Mother of the Nation’, and the Emirates Red Crescent Authority, among others.

She is the first woman to have been appointed to the Ministry of Justice in the UAE and has the honor of being a judicial authority in the administration of fatwas and legislation.

Dr. Amal received a doctorate in criminal law from Bielefeld University in Germany, and she has several published research and working papers, including her PhD thesis, which was on the penal code of juvenile delinquents, and a comparative study between the Anglo-Saxon and Latin systems.

Mahra Rashid Al Qubaisi

Sheltering and Care Department Manager

Mahra Rashid Al Qubaisi is the Sheltering and Care Department Manager at the Abu Dhabi Center for Shelter and Humanitarian Care – Ewaa.

She is one of the founding members with more than 12 years of experience in humanitarian work.

Mahra is responsible for implementing policies and outlining strategic directions related to survivors of violence, ensuring services provided to them are in accordance with the social sector agenda in Abu Dhabi and comply with the highest international standards.

Mahra previously held the position of director of the Support Services Department where she handled the financial, technical, procurement and human resources aspects. In addition, she worked as an operations officer at Dubai Islamic Bank and was one of the founders of the Jawhara Ladies Branch in Abu Dhabi, setting a precedent in the Islamic finance sector in the Emirate.

Mahra is also a member of several human rights committees, including the Family and Child Protection Committee of the Department of Community Development in Abu Dhabi and the National Committee to Combat Human Trafficking.

Mahra was awarded the Sheikh Rashid Award for Academic Excellence for her MBA degree from Zayed University. She has a Bachelor of Science degree in Business Administration from the Higher Colleges of Technology.

She holds a diploma in combating human trafficking crimes awarded by the Dubai Police General Command in cooperation with the National Committee to Combat Human Trafficking and has participated in the Child Protection Specialist Training Program organized by the Abu Dhabi Early Childhood Authority.

Mona Faisal Hussein

Director of the Support Services Department

As the Support Services Department Manager at the Abu Dhabi Center for Shelter and Humanitarian Care - Ewaa, Mona Faisal Hussein brings her extensive project management skills to the organization.

Since joining Ewaa in 2021, she is responsible for its administrative policies and practices related to Ewaa’s institutional support system. She leads the development of institutional performance, human resources, financial and technical affairs, procurement, facilities management, and public services.

Mona has more than two decades of experience. She has previously worked with Abu Dhabi General Services Company - Musanada, and the Dubai Statistics Center and the Abu Dhabi Company for Onshore Petroleum Operations Limited, which is associated with oil and gas giants such as Schlumberger and Total.

Mona holds a Master’s degree in Project Management from the George Washington University School of Business, and a Bachelor’s in computer engineering from Ajman University. She has successfully completed the Harvard Business School Leadership Excellence Program.

In addition to her academic qualification, she holds certifications in Engineering Data Management from the School of Data Management in Schlumberger, UK and Project Management from the Project Management Institute. She also trained in effective workforce planning and budget management from George Washington University and was a specialist in data management control for Total, France.

Building Capacity For Violence Prevention & Care

We recognize the importance of continuous learning in the area of violence prevention and care.

Across our integrated services, we support our employees with trainings and skills development. This includes risk and rescue assessment, psychological and medical care, legal and management follow-up, and other key skills and specializations. Learning can be in the form of certificates, hands-on trainings, programs, workshops, conferences, and diplomas.

Over the years, employees have prioritized learning in several related disciplines. These include:

  • Diploma in Combating Human Trafficking Crimes by the Dubai Police General Command in cooperation with the National Committee to Combat Human Trafficking.
  • Child Protection Specialist Training Program by Abu Dhabi Early Childhood Authority (ECA) in partnership with Georgetown University.
  • Social Care Certificate by Department of Community Development.
  • Train the Trainers program in the field of human trafficking awarded by Dubai Police General Command the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime.
